# Problem Statement Canvas: Maritime R&D AI Assistant (SDG 14)

This canvas defines the context, specific problem statements, target personas, and measurable gaps for the Maritime R&D AI Assistant project, aligned with **UN Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water**.

---

## 🌊 Context

The global economy relies heavily on massive tankers to transport crude oil. When structural failures, groundings, or collisions occur, the resulting spills are ecologically and economically catastrophic.

However, the maritime engineering industry is often stuck in legacy paradigms—attempting to solve dynamic, modern problems with traditional, brute-force physical engineering (e.g., *"let's just build thicker, heavier hulls"*). There is a wealth of cross-disciplinary scientific research and smart materials available, but bridging the gap between raw science and maritime application is incredibly slow.

### Systemic & Geopolitical Constraints (Crucial Reality Check)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Economic Dependency**: Global trade and energy systems are fundamentally tied to crude oil. Tankers must continue to operate at scale; halting oil transportation or forcing immediate transitions to green alternatives is economically impossible for the maritime industry today.

> [!WARNING]
> **Political & Regulatory Reality**: Phasing out fossil fuels is a multi-decade political process. The solution must ensure environmental safety (SDG 14) while keeping the current oil supply chain fully operational as-is. The AI engine must reject utopian proposals (like *"ban all tankers"*) and focus strictly on risk mitigation within the existing infrastructure.

---

## 🛑 Problem

Maritime R&D departments suffer from **"engineering blindness"** and data overload.

When tasked with mitigating oil spills without altering the core ship design or disrupting operations, engineers waste hundreds of hours manually sifting through unstructured patents, physics papers, and material science journals. They lack an intelligent system that can instantly:

* Cross-reference global research.
* Apply inventive problem-solving methodologies (like TRIZ to resolve technical contradictions).
* Generate explainable, evaluated concepts that respect strict real-world constraints (e.g., *"zero dry-docking required"*).

## 🧠 Emotional Affect

* Frustration: Stuck in endless, unproductive ideation loops that yield the same expensive, unviable physical solutions that fail late in the testing phase.
* Overwhelm: Drowning in the sheer volume of global scientific literature, unable to extract actionable mechanisms quickly while balancing strict maritime regulations (IMO).
* High-Stakes Anxiety: The cost of failure is immense. If her engineering solutions fail in the real world, the environmental and financial damage is irreversible.

## 📈 Measurable Gap

### 1. Time-to-Concept

It currently takes weeks or months of manual research to formulate a viable hypothesis, versus the minutes it should take with an AI-augmented RAG system.

### 2. Innovation Bottleneck

A high percentage of traditional ideas are rejected late in the process due to cost, weight, or operational constraints because technical contradictions weren't identified early enough.

### 3. The Explainability Deficit (Jury Focus)

Standard AI tools are rejected in hard engineering because they act as a **"black box."** The critical gap is the lack of a system that provides a transparent, source-backed *why* and *how* (Explainable AI) for every generated concept, along with automated constraint-checking.
